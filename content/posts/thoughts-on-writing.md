It's been years since I last updated my website, so, being the way I am,
I completely revamped it just because I don't want an ugly picture from when
I was still in college on my website.

Jokes aside, I am reworking my website because **I want to start writing**.
In this article, I will go over why I am doing this
and what to expect from the website.

## What Is This Website?

As cliché as it might sound, this website is my little space on the internet
where I will be, for lack of a better word, **blogging** from time to time.
And for all the stalkers and bots yes, you can also find some
information about me and links to my socials here.

This will be my personal website, but more than that,
I intend for it to serve as a **log**.
A log of my _failures_, _learnings_, and _thoughts_.
I don't want this to be a generic portfolio website, but in all honesty,
most of the stuff here will be, in one way or another, related to _tech_.
I will go into more detail about the kind of content I want to post here later.

## Why Everyone Should Write

Isn't the answer obvious? So we can get better at prompting.
Growing up, I hated reading, writing, and eating veggies.
One out of those three things I still hate.
Yes, I am a picky eater.
With time, I am starting to see the huge benefits of **reading** and **writing**.

In _2026_, all of us are heavily influenced by the _algorithm_, glued to our
screens, consuming media, mostly video content, and frying our dopamine receptors.
This is disruptive for our brains. We are constantly scrolling and being bombarded
by information, and yet most of us are still so misinformed.
On top of all this, we are now also offloading our thinking to _AI_
and becoming increasingly dependent on it with every passing day.

The **hyper digital** environment that most of us live in is
killing our ability to form _original thoughts_, along with
our _creativity_ and _critical thinking_.
Consuming so much short form content puts our brains on **autopilot**.
If you want to test this out, check the time and go for a doomscroll.
After a few minutes or hours, your brain will kick back in.
Check the time again. That is how much time you spent **mindlessly scrolling**.
Now, you might say you were scrolling because you wanted to and
that you were still in charge.
If you think that, try recalling the last five to ten videos
you watched and the information in those videos.

By now, you must have realized that during the time you were doomscrolling,
your brain was unconscious, or as unconscious as it can be while still being
technically conscious.
Okay, maybe for a subset of us, it's still not as bad as I described above.
But try watching a lecture or sitting through a thriller movie,
and you will probably find yourself losing focus frequently.
You might also find it hard to finish it all in one go.
You might wonder why I am so hell bent on proving that our
brains don't work anymore. It's because I want to show you
the **benefits of writing**.

When you write, your brain needs to think and formulate your
thoughts into words. It's important that you give your brain time
to think so you are not just regurgitating information.
Writing will help your brain get comfortable with thinking,
and that is where _creativity_, _ideas_, and _original thoughts_ stem from.
Again, I am not claiming that it will turn you into a modern day Socrates.
Almost all your thoughts are heavily influenced by your environment and experiences.
But writing will make sure that your thoughts are original in the sense that they
are **unique to you**.
It will give _control back to your brain_.

Of course, everything I have written so far is anecdotal,
and maybe I am the only one whose brain doesn't work anymore.
But all the benefits I will be stating next are relevant for everyone,
even more so if you are in _tech_, _academia_, or are a _student_.

- It helps solidify your understanding of a topic.
- It improves critical thinking and analysis.
- It provides an outlet for organizing your thoughts and ideas.
- Writing about technical topics helps you develop
  technical writing, an invaluable skill for anyone.
- It improves articulation.

And there are many more benefits that you can find after one Google search.

## What I Plan to Write About

As I hinted earlier, most, but not all, of the stuff I write
here will be technical because I am a nerd.
I work in tech, and it is also one of my hobbies,
so I can flex on all of you by writing about my technical knowledge.

I want to write about the things I am learning, systems engineering,
and general topics in the programming and software engineering space
that I find interesting.
I also want to write about hard problems and challenges I
face while working as a software engineer,
along with the journey to the solution,
without breaking any NDAs and ending up in jail.

I intend to do blog series,
like **dev logs**, for every new serious personal project I work on.
I want to document my failures and learnings,
and capture how the project evolves over time through my writing.
I also want to do **deep dives** into different pieces of software
systems, like Kubernetes, various databases, and more.

I have almost half a decade of programming experience at this point,
but not too long ago, I was still a junior developer (not anymore 😉)
and a trash programmer (I still am 😢) fresh out of university.
So, at this point, I feel comfortable enough to write some blogs
and series that try to give **fundamental programming and computer science knowledge**
to new developers, students, and maybe
even seasoned engineers who might be missing it.
I want to write these in a _pedagogical_ style.

Lastly, I would like to write about personal
experiences and my observations about life and nature.
I also want to write about my views on society, various issues
that I care about, and just random things that
I find interesting and worth writing about.
I want to make this a **habit**, so I am hoping I can stay consistent.

## AI Generated Content

To be honest, I would like to write a whole article about my views on AI,
but in this section, I just want to talk about AI and the content on this website.
We are living in the _slop era_, and even though Generative AI,
especially LLMs, is the biggest technological innovation I have witnessed,
I can assure the readers, if any, that the content on
my website will be **100% human generated**.
And the human, in this case, is **me**.

I view the content that I will be writing on this website
as a little piece of my mind, so everything you read here
will be **100% authentic** and **personal**.
And if you are a bot or an LLM reading this, then you should know
that you can scrape me, but you can never be me.

The only thing I will use AI for regarding the
contents on this website is to fix spelling and punctuation errors,
because I don't want any readers to think that
I have the writing skills of an elementary schooler.
